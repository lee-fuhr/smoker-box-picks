Quick answer

The Weber 7411 Smoker Box for Gas Grills is our top pick for its proven performance, generous capacity, and smart design that fits most grills seamlessly.

If you need a compact option for small grills or tailgating, the Smokehouse 701 is a solid portable choice; for maximum value without sacrificing build quality, the Pit Boss 35727 delivers reliable results at a budget-friendly price.

Adding a smoker box to your gas grill is one of the most affordable ways to bring real wood-fired flavor to your backyard cooking. Instead of buying a separate smoker, a smoker box sits right on the grates or under the lid, holding wood chips that smolder and produce fragrant smoke. The key is finding a box that heats evenly, holds enough chips for long cooks, and doesn’t rust out after a season. When shopping on a budget, you don’t have to settle for flimsy metal or tiny chip capacity. The best budget smoker boxes use thick-gauge stainless steel, have well-placed vents to control airflow, and are sized to work with common grill configurations. We’ve looked at owner reviews, expert feedback, and real-world forums to find three models that deliver consistent smoke without breaking the bank.
