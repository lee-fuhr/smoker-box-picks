Quick answer

The Weber 7411 Smoker Box is the best overall pick for reliable, even smoke on gas grills.

Budget, beginner, value, and heavy-duty users each have a dedicated winner: the Char Broil, Cuisinart, Pit Boss, and Masterbuilt, respectively.

A smoker box turns a standard gas grill into a wood-fired flavor machine, letting you infuse ribs, chicken, or vegetables with authentic smoke without buying a separate smoker. The key is matching the box to your grill size and cooking style: cast iron holds heat longest, stainless steel resists rust, and simple slide-top designs make refilling easier. When choosing, focus on three things: material (cast iron or stainless), size (must fit under your grates or sit on the flavorizer bars), and how easily you can add wood chips mid-session. A box that’s too small burns through chips too fast; one that’s too large can block airflow. Our picks range from budget-friendly basics to heavy-duty models that can handle back-to-back cooks.
